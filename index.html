About

I am a Research Assistant Professor at the Post-Metaverse Research Center, KAIST. I received my Ph.D. from the Graduate School of Culture Technology, KAIST (2022).

My research bridges screen-based media and immersive XR environments — exploring how existing stories and characters can be transformed into spatial, interactive experiences through advanced AI and user-centric design. I am particularly interested in designing intelligent systems that personalize story experiences in space, and bring media characters to life as interactive embodied AI agents in the real world.

I am open to collaboration opportunities. Please feel free to reach out via email.

Research Interests

  • Media Remediation in XR Transforming existing screen-based media into interactive AR/XR experiences that transcend the boundaries of the original content
  • Adaptive XR Storytelling Designing continuous narrative experiences across real-world locations — integrating spatial transitions and interaction outcomes into user-adaptive, personalized story flows
  • Embodied AI Interaction Bringing media characters to life as embodied conversational agents — enabling context-aware, multimodal interaction within XR environments
